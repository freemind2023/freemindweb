How to Publish a Book in India: Step-by-Step Guide for First-Time Authors

Publishing your first book in India can feel overwhelming — but it doesn't have to be. Whether you've written a novel, a self-help guide, a memoir, or an academic book, this step-by-step guide covers everything you need to go from completed manuscript to a book live on Amazon India.

What Does "Publishing a Book" Actually Mean?

Publishing a book means producing a finished, saleable product from your manuscript and making it available to readers through retail channels. It involves editing, design, formatting, ISBN registration, printing (for physical copies), and distribution to bookstores and online platforms.

In India, authors have three main publishing routes: traditional publishing (a publisher invests in your book in exchange for royalties), self-publishing (you manage and fund the process, keeping all royalties), and hybrid publishing (a middle ground where you contribute partially to costs in exchange for more support and better royalty terms than traditional publishing).

Step-by-Step: How to Publish a Book in India

1

Complete and Finalize Your Manuscript

Before anything else, your manuscript must be complete — not just a draft you plan to edit later. Write your full book, chapter by chapter, all the way to the end. Most first-time authors underestimate how much revision a manuscript needs. Plan for at least two rounds of self-editing before sending it to a professional editor.

Your manuscript should be in a standard word-processing format (Microsoft Word or Google Docs), double-spaced, with consistent chapter headings and no formatting distractions.

2

Professional Editing

Professional editing is the single most important investment you can make in your book. Readers — and especially reviewers — will forgive a simple cover before they forgive poor writing or inconsistent flow.

There are three types of editing you may need:

  • Developmental editing — Structure, pacing, character arcs, argument flow. For books with structural issues.
  • Copy editing — Sentence-level clarity, grammar, consistency, word choice. Every book needs this.
  • Proofreading — Final check for typos, punctuation, and formatting errors before going to print.

At Free Mind Consultancy, we provide all three levels as part of our book publishing services for authors.

3

Choose Your Publishing Route

Self-publishing is the most popular route for first-time authors in India today. Platforms like Amazon KDP, Notion Press, Pothi.com, and IngramSpark allow you to publish without a traditional publisher. You pay for production, but you keep 60–70% of royalties.

Traditional publishing requires submitting a query letter and manuscript to publishers. Acceptance rates are under 5% for unsolicited manuscripts. The process takes 12–24 months and you receive an advance (₹25,000–₹5,00,000 for most Indian authors) against royalties of 8–15%.

Real example: An Indian author selling 200 copies at ₹300 earns ₹36,000 via self-publishing (60% royalty) versus just ₹7,200 via traditional publishing (12%). Self-publishing recovers its upfront cost in 50–80 copies sold.
4

Get Your ISBN

An ISBN (International Standard Book Number) is a unique 13-digit identifier that every published book needs. In India, ISBNs are issued free of charge by the Raja Rammohun Roy National Agency for ISBN under the Ministry of Education.

You need separate ISBNs for each format (print, eBook, hardcover). The application is made online and typically takes 2–4 weeks. We handle this on your behalf as part of our publishing services.

5

Cover Design

Your book cover is your primary marketing asset. Research consistently shows that readers judge books by their covers — especially in an online marketplace like Amazon where your thumbnail competes with hundreds of other titles.

A professional cover design in India costs ₹5,000–₹25,000 depending on complexity. The designer will need to know your genre, target audience, and any specific visual direction. Your cover must be delivered in high-resolution formats suitable for both print (300 DPI, CMYK) and digital (RGB) use.

6

Interior Formatting

Interior formatting converts your edited manuscript into a print-ready PDF and/or an EPUB/MOBI file for digital distribution. Good formatting includes proper typography, chapter headings, page numbers, margins, and any images or tables.

Poorly formatted books are rejected by Amazon KDP quality review. We deliver both print-ready PDF files formatted to Amazon's specifications and EPUB files for Kindle and other eBook platforms.

7

Upload and Distribute

For self-publishing, upload your book to Amazon KDP for the largest reach. You'll need your formatted manuscript, cover file, ISBN, and book description (blurb). Set your price — most Indian English-language books sell for ₹199–₹499 in Kindle format and ₹299–₹799 in print.

Also consider distributing to: Flipkart (via Pothi or direct), Google Play Books, Apple Books (via Draft2Digital), and physical bookstores (via IngramSpark). We manage multi-channel distribution for all our publishing clients.

8

Market Your Book

Publishing is 50% producing the book and 50% marketing it. Before launch, build anticipation through your author social media. On launch day, run a coordinated push for reviews on Amazon (even 10–15 early reviews dramatically improve ranking).

Get featured on relevant podcasts to reach your target audience. Our podcast features service for authors handles all outreach and preparation. Post-launch, maintain visibility through content marketing, speaking engagements, and media coverage.

Pro Tip: The most common mistake first-time Indian authors make is rushing to publish without proper editing. A book with editing errors gets negative reviews that permanently damage sales and your author reputation. Invest in editing — it is always worth it.

How Long Does It Take to Publish a Book in India?

A realistic timeline for self-publishing in India:

  • Editing: 2–4 weeks (depending on manuscript length and number of rounds)
  • Cover design: 1–2 weeks
  • Interior formatting: 3–5 days
  • ISBN registration: 2–4 weeks (can run in parallel with editing)
  • Amazon KDP review: 24–72 hours
  • Total: 6–10 weeks from completed manuscript

What Does It Cost to Publish a Book in India?

Costs vary based on your manuscript length and the services you need:

  • Copy editing: ₹8,000–₹25,000
  • Cover design: ₹5,000–₹20,000
  • Interior formatting (print + digital): ₹3,000–₹8,000
  • ISBN registration: Free (government) or ₹1,500–₹3,000 through a service provider
  • Printing (if offset, 500 copies): ₹30,000–₹60,000
  • Total (without printing): ₹20,000–₹60,000

Frequently Asked Questions

How long does it take to publish a book in India?

Publishing a book in India typically takes 6 to 12 weeks from a completed manuscript. This includes editing, cover design, formatting, ISBN registration, and Amazon KDP review. Rush timelines of 3–4 weeks are possible for lightly edited manuscripts.

Do I need an ISBN to publish a book in India?

Yes. An ISBN is required for any book sold through retail channels including Amazon and physical bookstores. In India, ISBNs are issued free of cost by the Raja Rammohun Roy National Agency. The application takes 2–4 weeks.

Can I publish my book on Amazon from India?

Yes. Amazon Kindle Direct Publishing (KDP) is available to Indian authors. You can publish Kindle eBooks and print-on-demand paperbacks. eBook royalties are 35% or 70% depending on price. You need a formatted manuscript, cover, and ISBN.

What is the difference between self-publishing and traditional publishing in India?

Self-publishing means you handle all costs and keep all royalties. Traditional publishing means the publisher covers costs in exchange for majority royalties and some rights. Traditional publishing takes 1–2 years and requires a literary agent. Self-publishing is faster and gives you full control.
