Quick Answer

ISBN (International Standard Book Number) registration in India is handled by the Raja Rammohun Roy National Agency (RRRNAB). Any book intended for public sale, online retail (Amazon, Flipkart), or library distribution requires a unique 13-digit ISBN for each edition. Free Mind Consultancy manages the complete application, documentation, and follow-up — typically completed in 1-3 weeks.

Frequently Asked Questions — ISBN Registration India

What is ISBN registration and do I need it?

An ISBN (International Standard Book Number) is a unique 13-digit identifier required for any book intended for public sale, library cataloguing, or online retail distribution on Amazon, Flipkart, or bookstores. Without an ISBN, your book cannot be listed on major retail platforms or entered into library catalogues.

How long does ISBN registration take in India?

ISBN registration in India through the RRRNAB typically takes 1-3 weeks after submission of required documents — including manuscript details, author information, and publisher declaration. Free Mind manages the entire submission process and follow-up on your behalf.

Do I need a separate ISBN for print and digital editions?

Yes. Each edition of a book — paperback, hardcover, eBook, and audiobook — requires its own unique ISBN. Free Mind registers all required ISBNs simultaneously to save time and ensure complete distribution coverage.

Can a self-published author register an ISBN in India?

Yes. Self-published authors are fully eligible for ISBN registration in India. Free Mind registers your ISBN with you listed as both author and publisher, giving you complete ownership of the registration and your publishing rights.

Is a barcode the same as an ISBN?

No, but they are related. The EAN-13 barcode on the back of a print book is generated from the ISBN and represents it in scannable machine-readable format. Free Mind provides both the ISBN registration and the corresponding EAN-13 barcode file for your print edition cover.
